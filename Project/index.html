Mondrian images are iconic representations of modern art, characterized by their bold simplicity and striking use of primary colors. Inspired by Piet Mondrian’s abstract style, these works feature grids of black lines intersecting to form rectangles filled with red, blue, yellow, white, or left empty. Mondrian’s compositions, rooted in the De Stijl movement, seek harmony and balance, emphasizing the purity of geometric forms and the relationship between structure and color. They are celebrated as timeless expressions of minimalism, influencing art, design, and architecture worldwide.
